Sacraments

Baptism

You may be seeking to enquire about having your child baptised into the Catholic Church. Preparation sessions occur on the second and fourth Tuesday of each month. The fourth Tuesday of December and the second Tuesday of January are the exception as all enjoy the Christmas festive season and New Year holiday time.

Each child's baptism will require the parents to attend a once only preparation session where important details for church registration are gathered, information about the meaning of baptism is shared, a short DVD called 'My Baby's Baptism' is shown and discussed, and a possible date established for the baptism.

Please contact the parish office if you require further information or would like to give notice of your attending one of the sessions.

Baptisms are held every Sunday at 11:30 to noon at St. Mary's Kalgoorlie and as arranged at other mass centres

Preparation Sessions occur at St. Mary's Hall on the 2nd and 4th Tuesday of each month at 4pm. Please confirm your intention to attend on 9021 2100

Healing and Anointing of the sick

Whether you or a friend are at home, in a hospital or visiting the parish and are seriously ill, whether bodily or mental illness, please do not hesitate to contact the parish and request to receive the Sacrament of Anointing.

Once called the "The Last Rites", the sacrament is not only for those who may be facing death strengthening them and giving them courage, but is a sacrament of healing for those who may be seriously ill and could die. In receiving the sacrament, through the grace of Christ, they may be healed of life threatening injuries and or sickness.

Marriage

The parish greatly encourages your enquiry, if you and or your intended are Catholic and you desire to marry in any of our Catholic Churches in the Goldfields parish region, then you are warmly invited to enquire. You may also be seeking to marry at another parish Catholic Church and by a priest from elsewhere. The respective parties to be married (man and woman) will be required to attend marriage preparation sessions with the priest over several weeks and or months. The sessions include the preparation of civil and church documents.

Early contact with your parish priest and or the priest who will be the celebrant at your marriage is essential, even before you have booked a place for the reception or the church.

Please do not hesitate to contact the parish office if you are a Catholic and are seeking to marry.

Becoming Catholic

Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults and Children

The standard procedure for entering into and becoming a Catholic as an adult is through a period of time called the 'Catechumenate'. Saint Mary's Parish Kalgoorlie offers an RCIA/C program that begins in October and is completed at the Initiation ceremony at the Easter Vigil mass usually March/April. The fortnightly sessions are either weekly or fortnightly with a break during Christmas and the New Year.

If you are interested in this program, please contact the Parish Priest on 9021 2100
